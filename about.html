Our Story

From a side hustle to a community.

Like many resellers, our founder Kelsey Tetrick started small — selling online, learning the market, understanding what shoppers wanted. What she noticed was a problem: great sellers with beautiful inventory had nowhere to grow.

The options were stark: rent expensive retail space, stay online (limited reach, endless logistics), or give up 50%+ margins to consignment. None of them honored the work independent resellers pour into their collections.

So Kelsey built something different. A boutique that works for sellers instead of extracting from them. A space where sustainable fashion looks as good as it feels. A community where every piece — and every person selling it — is worth celebrating.

What We Stand For

Our values guide every decision.

Sustainability Over Speed

Fast fashion thrives on forgetting. We slow things down intentionally — curating pieces worth keeping, giving garments a second (and third) life. Every sale is one fewer item in a landfill.

Community Over Competition

Retold was built to lift up independent sellers, not compete with them. Our vendors aren't tenants — they're collaborators. When our community thrives, the whole store thrives.

Story Over Stuff

We're not in the business of moving volume. We're making connections — between a piece of clothing and the person it was made for next. Every item has a past. We help write its future.

Access Over Exclusivity

Sustainable fashion shouldn't be a luxury. Boutique shopping shouldn't require boutique prices. Small sellers shouldn't need deep pockets to reach real customers. Retold opens doors.

Meet the Founder

Kelsey Tetrick — reseller, tailor, community builder.

Kelsey brings a rare combination to Retold: a background as a Senior UX Designer with deep expertise in operations, business systems, and customer experience — paired with years as an active online reseller who knows how to source, price, merchandise, and move secondhand goods. She also does the alterations herself.

She understands this world from every angle — as a seller, a shopper, and a maker. Her vision goes beyond retail: building an ecosystem where Metro Detroit's independent resellers can find their footing, grow their reach, and connect with shoppers who genuinely care.

When she's not building the business, you'll find her hunting for vintage denim, perfecting a hem, or making the case that secondhand fashion is the most interesting chapter in style right now.
